Programs & Opportunities

Private Lessons

Private lessons are the foundation of Suzuki instruction. Students work one-on-one with their teacher (and parent, especially at early levels) to develop proper technique, musicality, and repertoire. Lessons are typically 30-60 minutes depending on the student's age and level.

Group Classes

Group classes are a vital component of the Suzuki Method. These classes provide opportunities for:

  • Playing with peers and developing ensemble skills
  • Reviewing and reinforcing repertoire in a fun, social setting
  • Learning from watching other students
  • Performing regularly in a supportive environment
  • Building community among students and families

Group classes are typically held monthly and are organized by instrument and level.

Workshops & Master Classes

Throughout the year, we host special workshops and master classes featuring:

  • Guest clinicians and renowned Suzuki teachers
  • Technique workshops for specific skill development
  • Chamber music opportunities
  • Performance preparation classes

Annual Spring Concert

Our annual Spring Concert is a celebration of our students' achievements, featuring performances from beginning students through advanced players. This event brings together the entire Pittsburgh Suzuki community for an inspiring day of music.

Summer Programs

Students have opportunities to participate in:

  • Local summer music camps
  • Suzuki institutes (regional and national)
  • Chamber music intensives

These programs offer intensive study, new friendships, and inspiration from the broader Suzuki community.

Scholarships

The Pittsburgh Suzuki Association offers limited scholarship assistance for families with financial need. Scholarships may be applied toward:

  • Workshop and event fees
  • Summer institute tuition
  • Instrument rental or purchase assistance

Contact us for information about scholarship applications and deadlines.
