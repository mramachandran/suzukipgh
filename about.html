About the Pittsburgh Suzuki Association

Our Mission

The Pittsburgh Suzuki Association exists to promote and support music education through the Suzuki Method in the greater Pittsburgh area. We provide resources, professional development, and community for teachers, students, and families committed to the Suzuki philosophy.

The Suzuki Method

The Suzuki Method, developed by Japanese violinist and educator Shinichi Suzuki, is based on the principle that all children can learn music in the same natural way they learn their native language. Key elements include:

  • Early beginning (students can start as young as age 3-4)
  • Parent involvement and partnership in the learning process
  • Listening to recordings daily
  • Constant repetition and review of repertoire
  • Learning in a positive, encouraging environment
  • Group classes in addition to private lessons
  • Delayed introduction of note reading

Our History

Founded in 1975, the Pittsburgh Suzuki Association has been serving families in the Pittsburgh region for nearly five decades. We have grown from a small group of dedicated teachers to a thriving community supporting hundreds of students each year across multiple instruments.

Instruments We Offer

Our member teachers provide instruction in:

  • Violin
  • Viola
  • Cello
  • Piano
  • Flute
  • Guitar
